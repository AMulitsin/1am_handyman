Got Questions?

FAQ — Fence & Gate Repair

Can you repair just part of my fence?
+
Yes — we repair individual boards, posts, and sections without touching the rest. Spot repairs are much cheaper than full replacement.
What wood do you use for repairs?
+
Pressure-treated lumber for posts (ground-contact rated). Cedar or pressure-treated pine for boards, matched to your existing fence.
My gate keeps sagging — can that be fixed?
+
Usually yes. Most sagging gates are fixed by replacing hardware, adding a tension rod, or re-hanging on a reinforced post. We'll diagnose on-site.
Do I need HOA approval?
+
Repairs that match existing fencing usually don't require HOA approval. Changing height, style, or color — check with your HOA first. We're happy to advise.
Can you build a completely new fence?
+
We build new fence sections and can handle full fence replacement. Contact us for a project estimate based on linear footage.
How do I know if my post needs replacing vs. just resetting?
+
A post that's leaning but solid can often be reset. One that's soft, cracked at ground level, or crumbling is ready for replacement. We'll show you what we find.
