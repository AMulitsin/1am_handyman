Got Questions?

FAQ — Minor Plumbing Repairs

Do I need to shut off the water before you arrive?
+
No — we handle that. We locate the shut-off for the specific fixture. No need to shut off the whole house.
Are parts and fixtures included?
+
Common small parts (supply lines, wax rings, flappers) are often on-hand. For larger fixtures like faucets and toilets, we recommend you choose and purchase the fixture, then we install it.
What counts as minor plumbing?
+
Fixture-level work — replacing or repairing items connected to existing supply and drain lines. We do not re-route pipes or do rough-in work, which requires a licensed contractor.
Is a permit required?
+
Most fixture replacements (faucets, toilets, disposals) do not require a permit in California. We'll let you know if your project might be an exception.
My toilet keeps running — can that be fixed?
+
Usually yes. A running toilet is almost always a faulty fill valve or flapper — a $10 part and 20 minutes. We diagnose and fix on the spot.
What if there's damage behind the wall?
+
If we find corroded pipes or hidden damage while doing fixture work, we stop and show you before going further. You always decide how to proceed.
