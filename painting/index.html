How It Works

01
Surface Prep
Holes filled, cracks caulked, glossy surfaces sanded for adhesion. Proper prep is what makes paint last 10 years instead of 3.
02
Protect & Tape
Floors covered with drop cloths, trim and outlets taped. Furniture moved away from walls and protected.
03
Prime & Paint
Bare drywall and repairs primed first. Two coats on walls changing color. Edges cut in by brush, rolled for even coverage.
04
Clean & Restore
Tape removed while paint is slightly tacky for clean lines. Drop cloths folded. Furniture moved back. Space handed over clean.

Got Questions?

FAQ — Painting

Do you supply the paint?
+
You can supply paint in your chosen color and finish, or we'll recommend what to buy — brand, sheen, and quantity. We carry all application tools.
How many coats do you apply?
+
Two coats on walls changing color, one coat for same-color touch-ups. All bare drywall and repairs are primed first.
How long does a room take?
+
A standard bedroom takes 4–6 hours including prep and cleanup. Rooms with trim take longer. We give a realistic estimate before starting.
Do you move furniture?
+
We move lightweight furniture away from walls and protect everything with drop cloths. Large pieces (sofas, beds) are better moved beforehand if possible.
Can you match an existing paint color?
+
Bring us a chip from the wall or the original paint can and we'll match it. Most hardware stores can scan existing paint for an exact match.
Do you repair walls before painting?
+
Yes — we fill nail holes, caulk cracks, and sand rough areas as part of normal prep. Larger drywall repairs are quoted separately if needed.
