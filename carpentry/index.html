How It Works

01
Assess & Measure
We assess the scope — what's damaged, what can be repaired vs. replaced, what material is needed. Measurements taken before any cuts.
02
Material Selection
We specify the right wood grade, treatment, and profile for the application. Rot repairs use pressure-treated lumber where needed.
03
Cut, Fit & Fasten
Pieces cut to fit, fastened with proper hardware — screws, nails, or construction adhesive depending on the joint type.
04
Finish & Sand
Exposed edges sanded smooth, caulk gaps where trim meets wall, surface left paint-ready or stained per request.

Got Questions?

FAQ — Carpentry

Do you supply wood and materials?
+
Small hardware and fasteners are on-hand. For lumber we'll specify exactly what to buy — right grade, treatment, and quantity. Materials at cost, no markup.
Can you match existing woodwork?
+
We match profiles, styles, and finishes as closely as possible. We discuss what's achievable before starting.
What's the difference between rot repair and full replacement?
+
Rot repair means cutting out the damaged section and splicing in new wood — typically 60–80% cheaper than full replacement and structurally sound.
Can you build new fence sections?
+
Yes — new fence sections built to match existing height, style, and spacing. Full new fence construction quoted on a project basis.
Do you do custom shelving?
+
Yes — we design and build custom shelving for closets, garages, and living spaces. Bring us your dimensions and we'll build it.
My door is sticking — is that a big job?
+
Usually not. Sticking doors are commonly fixed in 30–60 minutes by planing, shimming hinges, or adjusting the strike plate.
